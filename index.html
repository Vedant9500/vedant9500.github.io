03. Process

Build first, understand deeper

This is where the homepage becomes more than a repository list. It should explain how ideas turn into projects and why the work feels coherent.

Notebook summary

Good projects start with a technical question that can be tested.

  • Pick a problem where the behavior can be seen, measured, or explained clearly.
  • Build the smallest honest version first, then improve the structure and presentation.
  • Keep the result discussable in interviews, not just technically interesting in isolation.
01. Start with behavior

Prefer ideas that produce something observable.

Models, simulations, and tools all have one advantage in common: they give you something tangible to evaluate instead of vague claims.

02. Build the honest version

Clarity matters more than looking advanced too early.

The strongest portfolio pieces are the ones where the idea, build, and outcome are easy to follow without extra decoding.

03. Refine the explanation

Make the project legible to someone else.

A good homepage does not just show work. It gives enough framing that a recruiter or interviewer can understand why each project is here.

04. Writing

The blog lives separately now.

The homepage should scan quickly. Research notes, experiments, and archive browsing belong on the blog page, where the writing can breathe without competing with the main portfolio story.
